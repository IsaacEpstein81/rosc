Executive Summary

Flagship-first rollout, sequenced for practical deployment.

Phase 1 is designed around a disciplined order of operations: provider-facing infrastructure first, client engagement second, and future system-of-record exploration as a separate strategic choice.

Strategic Future Workstream

Lightweight, recovery-focused system of record

During Phase 1, ROSC and OpenRecovery will use live working sessions to clarify the requirements, boundaries, and implementation path for a future recovery-centered record system.

Why It Matters

A recovery-focused system of record could become the connective layer across inpatient treatment, outpatient support, recovery coaching, and community engagement in a way traditional EMRs often do not.

Continuity across settings Recovery-centered workflows Provider visibility over time Better handoffs and follow-up Clear ownership of key data

During Phase 1 We Will Discuss

  • Which workflows should move from inpatient to outpatient to community
  • What the future record should own versus what remains in existing systems
  • Documentation, compliance, and Canadian operational realities
  • The minimum viable scope for an initial version of the product

What Phase 1 Should Produce

  • A clearer product definition for a lightweight recovery record
  • Prioritized workflow requirements and system boundaries
  • An implementation sequence and decision-ready roadmap
  • Alignment on whether ROSC wants to advance into build scope next

Build Later, Plan Now

The goal in Phase 1 is not to launch this system of record immediately. The goal is to make it concrete enough that ROSC finishes the sprint with a practical plan for what to build, how to sequence it, and what should happen in a follow-on implementation phase.

Implementation Considerations

Keep the rollout practical now, then scale from a successful flagship prototype.

Flagship launch complexity

Launching a new 100-bed facility while introducing new infrastructure creates change-management risk.

Mitigation: keep Phase 1 focused on core provider and engagement workflows that are pilot-ready.

Training bottlenecks

Staff readiness and supervisory capacity can quickly become growth constraints.

Mitigation: start with competency-based training, roleplay, and supervisor visibility.

Customization creep

Too much bespoke work can slow scaling and reduce repeatability.

Mitigation: use standardized product foundations with targeted ROSC-specific configuration.

Knowledge-base quality

Copilot performance depends on the quality of approved source materials.

Mitigation: start with high-priority approved artifacts and expand iteratively.

Recovery-record scope creep

Mixing current deployment with a larger product design effort can slow both.

Mitigation: keep the recovery-record concept separate and decision-based.

Expansion timing

Rolling out too early across multiple sites can dilute quality at the flagship facility.

Mitigation: treat the flagship deployment as the prototype, then expand in phases.
